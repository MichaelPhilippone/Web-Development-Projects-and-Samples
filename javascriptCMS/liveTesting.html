Consensus Decision Making

In ICES, we try hard to give everyone an equal chance to have input. Consensus-based decision making helps us ensure that this happens. Consensus involves a lot of listening, so when we're making a decision together, we'll go around in a circle and have everyone say what they think we should do. From there, we'll discuss everyones' points and eventually reach a decision everyone is happy with. We prefer to make decisions this way, as opposed to "majority rules," because we think it's important for everyone to feel happy with the choices we make. Listening also helps us bond and get to know each other better, and helps foster respect. Ultimately, it makes the group stronger. And a stronger ICES is a happier ICES!

 

Core Group

Core Group is open at all times to anyone. We meet once a week outside of ICES to discuss how things are going within the club, prepare for the next regular meeting, talk with our advisor, and handle the paperwork and bureaucracy required to keep us afloat. If you're interested in becoming more active, learning about leadership, or just plain want to know more, you're welcome to come! Talk to one of the Executive Board folks (below) to find out when and where Core Group meets.
